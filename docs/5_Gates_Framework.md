# The 5 Gates of AI Product Launch

## Why this framework exists

Launching AI products is fundamentally different from launching traditional software.

Traditional software follows deterministic rules. AI systems produce probabilistic outputs, evolve over time, and introduce new risks around trust, safety, cost, and governance.

Many AI teams focus heavily on model performance but struggle with one critical question:

> **"Are we actually ready to launch?"**

The **5 Gates of AI Product Launch** is a practical framework that helps product teams answer that question consistently.

It is designed for AI Product Managers, Engineering Managers, Applied AI teams, and business stakeholders who need a structured way to evaluate launch readiness.

---

# The Five Gates

## Gate 1 — Customer Value

### Guiding Question

**Are we solving a meaningful customer problem?**

Before evaluating models, ensure the product creates value.

### Evidence

* Customer problem validated
* Target persona identified
* User journey documented
* Success metrics defined
* Business objective agreed upon

### Supporting Artifacts

* AI PRD
* Customer Journey
* Product Strategy

---

## Gate 2 — AI Quality

### Guiding Question

**Is the AI consistently good enough?**

AI quality is more than accuracy.

Teams should evaluate:

* Correctness
* Grounding
* Helpfulness
* Latency
* Cost
* Hallucination rate
* Escalation behavior

### Supporting Artifacts

* Evaluation Plan
* Golden Dataset
* Example Evaluation Plan
* AI Evaluation Workbench

---

## Gate 3 — Trust & Safety

### Guiding Question

**Can customers trust the system?**

AI should behave safely, transparently, and consistently.

Consider:

* Privacy
* Bias
* Prompt injection
* Harmful outputs
* Policy compliance
* Responsible AI review

### Supporting Artifacts

* Risk Register
* Responsible AI Notes
* Launch Readiness Framework

---

## Gate 4 — Operational Readiness

### Guiding Question

**Can the organization operate and support this feature?**

Even high-quality AI products fail without operational readiness.

Verify:

* Monitoring dashboards
* Escalation workflows
* Incident response
* Support documentation
* Rollback plan

### Supporting Artifacts

* Launch Readiness Framework
* Executive Decision Memo

---

## Gate 5 — Business Readiness

### Guiding Question

**Will this create measurable business value?**

Every AI launch should contribute to meaningful business outcomes.

Evaluate:

* Expected ROI
* Adoption
* Cost per interaction
* Customer satisfaction
* Operational efficiency

### Supporting Artifacts

* Executive AI Decision Memo
* Business Metrics
* Rollout Plan

---

# Putting It All Together

A product should pass all five gates before progressing to production.

| Gate | Primary Question                    | Key Artifact               |
| ---- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| G1   | Does this solve a customer problem? | AI PRD                     |
| G2   | Is the AI good enough?              | Evaluation Plan            |
| G3   | Can customers trust it?             | Risk Register              |
| G4   | Can we operate it?                  | Launch Readiness Framework |
| G5   | Does it create business value?      | Executive Decision Memo    |

---

# How to Use This Playbook

A typical product lifecycle using this repository looks like:

1. Define the problem using the AI PRD.
2. Create an Evaluation Plan.
3. Validate quality using the Evaluation Workbench.
4. Assess launch readiness.
5. Review risks.
6. Prepare an Executive Decision Memo.
7. Make a Go / No-Go launch decision.

---

# Guiding Principle

> Great AI products are not defined by impressive demos.
>
> They are defined by disciplined product thinking, measurable quality, responsible governance, and continuous learning.
